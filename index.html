A short story about each season

1 Season

Chemistry teacher Walter White of the American city of Albuquerque is diagnosed with lung cancer. Doctors believe that he has about two years left to live. In order to provide money for Skyler’s pregnant wife and son of Walter Jr. before dying, White begins to cook and sell meth with his former apprentice Jesse Pinkman and his friends Badger and Skinny Pete. Amphetamine brewed by Walter and Jesse is superior in quality to the competition. White contacts the drug dealer Tuko Salamanca and becomes known among other dealers as Heisenberg. Walter's brother-in-law, Hank, works in the Drug Enforcement Department (OBN) and investigates the Heisenberg case and thus goes to Salamanca.

2 Season

In a fit of paranoia, Tuco kidnaps Walter and Jesse, thinking that it was they who could surrender his OBN. In the end, the drug dealer is killed by the hunter Hank. Walter and Jesse are running away at this moment. The main characters return to Albuquerque. Together with her, Jesse first tries heroin. Walter is in remission, and the tumor has reacted to chemotherapy. The main characters meet lawyer Sol Goodman - he offers them an alternative way of doing business and connects with a major trader Gustavo Fring. Fring uses his legal business, a fast food chain, as a cover. Jane dies from an overdose, although White, who was nearby at that moment, could save her. Jesse is inconsolable. Saul Goodman calls a cleaner named Mike, who hides evidence and helps Jesse talk to the police.

3 Season

Walter, under pressure from Skyler, tells her about meth production. She in response admits that she was sleeping with her boss - this infuriates White. Skyler leaves Walter and wants to get a divorce, now the couple live separately. Jesse after a quarrel with his mentor begins independent drug production. At the same time, the twins from the Salamanca family announce a hunt for White, they blame him for the death of Tuko Salamanca. Soon, Jesse returns to work at Fring. Jesse begins dating a girl named Andrea. Fring claims that Walter should work with obedient Gale instead of the unstable rebel Jesse. Walter is afraid that Gail will reveal the secrets of producing high-quality meta - and then he and Jesse can be eliminated.

4 Season

Jesse tormented conscience over the murder of Gale. To think less about it, he uses drugs in large quantities. Hank is returning home; he has not fully recovered after being injured, but continues to investigate. Gustavo Fring is in conflict with the drug cartel, for which Jesse must cook meth in Mexico. Mike, Jesse and Gustavo go to Mexico. Walter stays in Albuquerque. All three return to Albuquerque. Gustavo tells Walter that Hank will have to be killed if he gets too close to revealing their business. Then Geisenberg tries to poison the girl’s son Jesse and convinces his student that this is the work of Gustavo (in order to enlist the help of his partner). Fortunately, the child survives. At the end of the season, Gustavo dies from the explosion. Of course, Walter set this trap.

5 Season

Jesse and Walter make connections with former assistant Gustavo Lydia and hire a man named Todd. Together with him, they steal methylamine, which is required for the production of methamphetamine, from the train. The cleaner Mike, who worked for Gustavo Fring, is reached by the BSS. They ask to hand over his accomplices. Walter has to shoot Mike. Walter turns to Uncle Todd, the leader of a neo-Nazi gang. He organizes the killing of people who had business with Geisenberg - who, after the death of Gustavo, can speak. Jesse realizes that it was White who poisoned his girlfriend's son - and begins to work closely with Hank. Then the former chemistry teacher asks the neo-Nazi leader to remove Jesse. Neo-Nazis kill Hank and his partner, and Jesse is captured so that he continues to cook high-quality meth. Walter shoots neo-Nazis from an automatic machine gun installed in the trunk of a car (he himself is injured). Jesse runs off in a car. The last frames show the dying Heisenberg.

Director

Vince Gilligan is best known as the creator, screenwriter, director and producer of the drama series Breaking Bad on AMC. For work on this series, Gilligan was nominated six times for the prestigious Emmy Award from 2008 to 2012. The series itself was nominated for this award four times and won in 2013. As a producer, Gilligan also received one figurine. In the same year, the US Scriptwriters Guild placed "Breaking Bad" on the thirteenth place in the list of the best series in history. In addition, Gilligan was twice awarded the Screenwriting Guild of the United States for his work on the script. Also in 2013, the series was listed in the Guinness Book of Records as the series with the highest rating. The series finale took place on September 29, 2013 and gathered an audience of 10.3 million viewers at the screens.
